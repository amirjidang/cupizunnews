American film and TV actor James Caviezel has an estimated net worth of $25 million. He is best recognized for playing the title character in the film The Passion of the Christ. He is also noted for his appearance in several notable film projects which include Bobby Jones: Stroke of Genius, The Thin Red Line, Déjà Vu, Frequency, and The Count of Monte Cristo. He also starred in Person of Interest, a crime drama series aired on CBS.  

James Patrick Caviezel, Jr. was born on September 26, 1968, in Mount Vernon, Washington, U.S. He is of Romansh, Swiss, Slovak and Irish ancestry. Since 1996, he has been married to Kerri Browitt. He has three children named Bo, Lyn, and David. After graduating from John F. Kennedy Memorial High School, Caviezel attended Bellevue Community College. On his second year, he had a foot injury which put an end to his future in the NBA. Eventually, he moved to the University of Washington and decided to focus more on acting.

Some of Caviezel's most notable film projects include My Own Private Idaho, The Passion of Christ, Wyatt Earp, Diggstown, Children of the Dust, Ed, The Rock, G.I. Jane, Ride with the Devil, Pay It Forward, Madison, High Crimes, Highwaymen, I Am David, Déjà Vu, The Stoning of Soraya M., Outlander, The Prisoner, Nature's Grave, Generator Rex, Transit, Escape Plan, and Savannah.
