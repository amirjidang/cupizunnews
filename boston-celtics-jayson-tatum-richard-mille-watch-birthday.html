Boston Celtics Jayson Tatum Buys $500,000 Richard Mille Watch For Birthday

Publish date: 2024-08-15

Boston Celtics star Jayson Tatum went all out for his 24th birthday, as he treated himself to a $500,000 Richard Mille watch.

According to TMZ, Tatum consulted celebrity jeweller Eric Da Jeweler to create the custom piece. The outlet notes that the all-white watch featured a “skeletonized movement and a grade 5 titanium case band and back bezel.”

Sharing with his 317,000 Instagram followers, Da Jeweler shared a series of photos featuring Tatum’s new watch. The first image shows the jeweller and athlete posing together at a restaurant. Dressed in an all-white outfit, Tatum can be seen wearing the white watch on his left wrist.

A second photo in the slideshow gives an up-close look at the watch. With a completely transparent face, all of the watch’s mechanical insides are in view. The pieces features silver and black hardware, with the Richard Mille name visible in small, white lettering on the top of the watch’s face.

“Happy birthday to my guy @jaytatum0,” Da Jeweler wrote in the caption, revealing he delivered the custom piece to the basketball player himself in Boston.

“Had to make a special delivery in Boston with that all white Richard Mille RM055 Bubba Watson! Half a million well spent #Investment #JaysonTatum #AllWhiteEverything,” the jeweller continued alongside a money bag emoji.

Richard Mille is a Swiss luxury watch company, and their pieces certainly don’t come cheap. According to Wrist Advisor, Richard Mille watches can range anywhere from $60,000 to $1.3 million. Factors like the material, complications, and whether it’s limited edition all affect the price of the designer brand’s watches.

Half a million dollars may seem like a huge chunk of change to spend on a watch, but perhaps not for someone who signed a contract worth almost $200 million recently.

In November 2020, Tatum agreed to a five-year contract extension with the Boston Celtics valued at $195 million. But it’s suspected the deal could increase to $200 million in coming years. TMZ notes that Tatum has already made $57 million since joining the Celtics in 2017.

Even though Tatum is now wearing half a million dollars on his wrist, that may not have been his best birthday gift this year. On Thursday, the athlete shared a sweet video of his 4-year-old son taking him to his favourite spots in his hometown of St. Louis, Missouri.

Tatum later uploaded images from a birthday celebration, with the first photo showing the basketball star embracing his toddler. The background is decorated with a variety of balloons, including orange and black ones designed to look like basketballs. It’s clear that Tatum had a good time celebrating his 24th!
