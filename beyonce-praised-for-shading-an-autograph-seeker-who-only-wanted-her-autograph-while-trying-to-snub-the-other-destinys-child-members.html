Beyoncé Still Handed Over The Book To Her Other Destiny's Child Members After A Fan Requested Only Her Autograph

Approaching a celebrity on the street can be risky, especially when seeking an autograph. However, at meet and greets, the vibe is generally positive. A fan tried to change that completely, going out of his way to get Beyoncé's signature, while snubbing the other members of Destiny's Child. He makes his intentions clear with Beyoncé, but the artist wasn't having it.

The fan says, "Just yours," while Beyoncé responds, "Alright." After she's done signing, Beyoncé proceeds to pass the book over to the Destiny's Child members regardless. You can even see the fan trying to get the book back, but Bey wasn't having it.

The clip also shows Michelle Williams sensing something was up during the moment, knowing Beyoncé had just shaded a fan for rude behavior.

The moment was caught, and it caused quite a stir.

Fans were clearly content with the way Beyoncé handled the moment. Still to this day, she holds her time with the group in high regard, and treats her fellow members with nothing but love and respect.

Beyoncé Still Treats Destiny's Child With The Utmost Respect

Looking back at their reunion, LeToya Luckett revealed Beyoncé created a wonderful space for the Destiny's Child reunion.

The artist revealed alongside People, “I'm so glad that [Beyoncé] created a wonderful space for us to all come together,” Luckett says. “[The] love in the room was everything that I wanted it to be, thought it could be,” she shares. “It was what I wanted and always dreamed of. And I'm blessed to have even had that opportunity because there's some people that'll never get that.”

She continues, “Everybody's just going on to do amazing things,” she says. “To also see everybody as moms and wives, we've grown up. We're adults out here, it's full-blown adulting going on.”

As for Beyoncé and her thoughts on Destiny's Child, the artist admitted in the past that she was "underestimated" in the group.

"I'd say I discovered my power after the first Destiny's Child album. The label didn't really believe we were pop stars," Beyonce shares. "They underestimated us, and because of that, they allowed us to write our own songs and write our own video treatments. It ended up being the best thing, because that's when I became an artist and took control."

She continues, "So we created it on our own, and once it was successful, I realized that we had the power to create whatever vision we wanted for ourselves," she adds. "We didn't have to go through other writers or have the label create our launch plans -- we had the power to create those things ourselves."

Beyoncé's respect holds true for the group until this very day, and it is great to see how much faith she always had, despite the lack of belief from others regarding the group's vision.
