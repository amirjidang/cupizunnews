Who Is Sandra Cleveland, Glenda Clevelands Daughter?

Publish date: 2024-08-05

Sandra Cleveland is the daughter of Glenda Cleveland. They are neighbors of Jeffery Dahmer; Glenda Cleveland was the first one in society who suspected that there was something with this house and boy.

She called many times to police in the area about unusual activities happening, but the police did get her seriously. Somehow can say due to racism but their ignorance comes to a harsh tragedy in history.

In the Netflix monster series on Jeffery Dahmer, they are both somehow ignored;  they are the central part of capturing him. Let’s take a look at Sandra Cleveland, Glenda Cleveland’s daughter how he played a significant role in the monster story;

Who is Sandra Cleveland or Sandra Smith?

Glenda Cleveland, who lived next door to Jeffrey Dahmer, had been worried about the former soldier’s behavior for several months.

According to the documentary Monster: The Jeffrey Dahmer Story, which is available on Netflix, Cleveland had excellent reason to assume that something was not quite right due to the sound of men screaming in pain, the sound of a drill at all hours of the night, and a bad smell.

On May 27, 1991, Cleveland called the police to Dahmer’s apartment after her daughter, Sandra Smith, and her niece, Nicole Childress, discovered a kid bleeding, naked, and unable to communicate on the street. 

Cleveland believed that Dahmer was responsible for the boy’s condition. According to the Milwaukee Journal Sentinel, the authorities discovered him in an alleyway while fleeing from Dahmer.

On the other hand, Dahmer persuaded the police that the youngster was 19 years old and that the two of them were involved in a relationship. Dahmer informed the police that the boy had been drinking and that they had gotten into an argument before the incident.

Later, it was discovered that the youngster was Konerak Sinthasomphone, who was only 14 years old and became Dahmer’s 13th victim.

A few days later, when Cleveland saw a photo of Konerak in a missing person alert published in the newspaper, she recognized that he was the young kid Dahmer had claimed to be his partner. She tried to get in touch with the police again, but no one answered her call. She even tried to get in touch with the FBI, but no one responded to her.

According to The Sun, five of Dahmer’s 17 murders, including that of Konerak, were carried out after Cleveland began calling police about her concerns. One of these murders was carried out after Cleveland began contacting the police.

According to The Associated Press, the three officers Cleveland summoned in May 1991 were suspended without pay pending an internal investigation after the arrest of Dahmer and the discovery of Konerak’s remains. Dahmer had been charged with the murder of Konerak.

Niecy Nash’s thoughts, how played her role in Series?

 In an interview with Netflix, Niecy Nash, who plays the role of Cleveland in the Series, stated that Cleveland was also a victim of Jeffrey Dahmer and that her tale needed to be told.

She stated, “When you hear the different things that happen with regard to Jeffrey Dahmer and his victims… Glenda was one of his victims as well, and her story has been told the least of these.” In addition to this, she stated, “Heavy is the head that wears the crown in order to tell this story.”

In her words: “Glenda Cleveland was a remarkable and unique individual. If she was going to keep going on and on and on in an effort to convince someone to do something, then she deserved a lot more recognition than a cheesy plaque tucked away at the back of some social hall. 

She deserved far more than for the cops to stand in front of her and say, “Look what we did, look what we tried to do,” and I want people to know that we have all known a Glenda Cleveland in this life, or will know one in the future, or will have been one in the past. That much is certain.”

Sandra Cleveland’s thoughts after her mother’s death?

On December 24, 2010, Glenda Cleveland died due to natural causes. She had 56 years under her belt.

Her daughter Sandra Smith informed the Milwaukee Journal Sentinel that after Dahmer’s arrest in 1991, Cleveland moved into the Oxford Apartments, located on the same block where Dahmer had lived. She remained there until 2009.

She was given recognition by Milwaukee’s Common Council and County Board, and Mayor John Norquist referred to her as a “model citizen” in his speech. In addition, she was honored with accolades from local women’s organizations and the Milwaukee Police Department.

Cleveland maintained his relationship with the Sinthasomphone family, even going so far as to attend the wedding of one of the boys.
