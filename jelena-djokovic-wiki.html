Jelena Djokovic (Novak Djokovics Wife): Wiki, Age, Parents, Net Worth, Kids, Height &amp; Biography

Publish date: 2024-08-07

Jelena Djokovic is a Serbian entrepreneur, humanitarian, and the wife of famous tennis player Novak Djokovic. She is the co-founder and global director of the ‘Novak Djokovic Foundation,’ a charity that works for underprivileged children in Serbia.

She is also the founder and editor-in-chief of ‘Original Magazine,’ a digital platform that features stories of celebrities and influencers from various fields. Jelena is a multi-talented woman who has a passion for writing, reading, nutrition, sports, and animals. She is also a loving mother of two children and a supportive partner of her husband.

Biography and Education

Jelena was born as Jelena Ristic on June 17, 1986 (Age: 37 years old) in Belgrade, Serbia. She is the daughter of Miomir and Vera Ristic and has an elder sister named Marija Ristić. Jelena attended a sports high school in Belgrade where she met her future husband Novak Djokovic. They started dating in 2005 when Novak was 18 years old and Jelena was 19 years old.

Jelena Djokovic was a studious girl who dreamed of studying abroad. She worked hard to get scholarships and enrolled at ‘Bocconi University’ in Milan where she graduated with a degree in business administration in 2008. She then pursued a master’s degree in luxury management from the ‘International University of Monaco’ and completed it in 2011. Her thesis was on the impact of social media on luxury brands.

Career and Achievements

Jelena began her career as an intern for the ‘Oilinvest Group,’ a European energy provider from January to August 2009. She then co-founded her own digital marketing and brand development firm ‘Jelena Ristic Consulting’ in July 2011 and became its director. She also launched ‘Original Magazine’ in 2012 as a project of the ‘Novak Djokovic Foundation.’ The magazine covers topics such as fashion, sports, lifestyle, health, and wellness and features interviews with celebrities and influencers from around the world.

Jelena is also actively involved in philanthropic work through the ‘Novak Djokovic Foundation,’ which was established by her husband in 2007. The foundation aims to provide quality education and basic amenities to underprivileged children in Serbia. Jelena served as the global CEO of the foundation from August 2012 to September 2016 and is currently the global director. She has been recognized for her humanitarian efforts and has received awards such as the ‘Humanitarian Award’ by ‘Harper’s Bazaar’ in 2014 and the ‘Best Humanitarian Project Award’ by ‘Grazia’ magazine in 2016.
