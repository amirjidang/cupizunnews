Who is Christian Navarro?

Christian Lee Navarro was born on 21 August 1991 under the sign of Leo, in The Bronx, New York City USA, of Puerto Rican ancestry, but mixed ethnic group, and is American by nationality. He is a 28-year-old actor, probably best recognized for starring in the role of Jorge in the HBO period drama series “Vinyl” (2016), playing Tony Padilla in the Netflix teen drama mystery series “13 Reasons Why” (2017-2019), and featuring as Kurt in the biographical comedy crime film “Can You Ever Forgive Me?” (2018).

How rich is he, as of now? Christian Navarro Net Worth

As of early 2020, Christian Navarro’s net worth is over $2 million, acquired through his successful involvement in the film industry as a professional actor, who has starred in more than ten television and film titles since his career started in 2005.

Early Life, Nationality, Ethnicity, and Educational Background

Christian Navarro spent his early years in The Bronx, raised by his parents in a Christian household – his father is C. L. Navarro, while the name of his mother hasn’t been disclosed yet. Christian has a younger sister, whose name is Leeann Madisyn Navarro.

Christian developed his interests in acting very early, and while in high school, he joined the school’s drama program and performed in various productions. He has a degree in Arts and Literature.

Career Beginnings

Christian Navarro started his acting career at the age of 14. In the very beginning, he made appearances in various low budget film titles, and advertising commercials. His debut film appearance came in 2005, when he landed a small role of The Infected in the horror film “Day Of The Dead 2: Contagium”, which was the unofficial prequel to the 1985 zombie horror film, entitled “Day Of The Dead”.

Two years later, Christian guest-starred as Paco Mendoza in the episode “Senseless” of the NBC police procedural drama series “Law & Order: Criminal Intent”, and then appeared as himself in the comedy film “The Doorman”, directed by Wayne Price.

Guest-Starring Appearances

In 2009, Christian made his feature film debut landing the role of John White in the crime drama film “Run It”, directed by Evangelos Giovanis and George Giovanis. He then guest-starred in the episode “To Protect And Serve” of the CBS police procedural drama series “Blue Bloods” in 2013.

In 2014, Christian continued to land guest-starring roles in a number of TV series, appearing as Waiter Bobby in the episode “Double Identity” of the NBC action-comedy police procedural series “Taxy Brooklyn”, and played Jed in the episode “1.8” of the Showtime drama series “The Affair”. Afterwards, in 2015 he portrayed Carlos Gomez in the pilot episode of another police procedural drama series, “Rosewood” on Fox.

Recurring Role, “Vinyl”, and “Bushwick”

Christian Navarro won his debut recurring role in 2016, when he was chosen to play Jorge in the period drama web series “Vinyl”, created by Rich Cohen, Martin Scorsese, Terence Winter and Mick Jagger, and aired on the HBO network from February to April 2016. In the next year, Christian made a guest-starring appearance as Sidekick in the pilot episode of the Amazon Video series “The Tick”, which was followed by his portrayal of Eduardo in the action thriller film “Bushwick”, directed by Cary Murnion and Jonathan Milott.

Rise to Prominence and “13 Reasons Why”

Christian Navarro came to prominence in 2017, when he joined the cast of the teen drama mystery series “13 Reasons Why”, portraying the main role of Tony Padilla, appearing with such actors as Dylan Minnette and Katherine Langford. The series is developed by Brian Yorkey and produced by Selena Gomez, and is based on the 2007 novel of the same title written by Jay Asher. It premiered on 31 March 2017 on Netflix, and is currently in its fourth and final season.

In 2018, he received an Imagen Award nomination in the category of Best Actor – Television for his performance in the series. Side by side, Christian landed the role of Kurt in the biographical comedy crime film “Can You Ever Forgive Me?” in 2018, directed by Marielle Heller, and featuring Richard E. Grant, Melissa McCarthy and Dolly Wells. Christian won the Rising Star Award at the 2018 San Diego International Film Festival.
