Peso Pluma Parents &amp; Family Details

Publish date: 2024-08-17

Hassan Emilo Kabande Laija, better known as Peso Pluma, is a rising star in the music industry, known for his unique blend of classic Mexican songs, modern pop, and R&B beats. 

While much is known about Peso Pluma’s impressive career and discography, very little is known about his personal life, including his parents.

Early Influences

Born on June 15, 1999, in Zapopan, Jalisco, Mexico, Peso Pluma grew up in a musically inclined family with parents from the neighboring states of Sinaloa and Jalisco.

While his parents’ identities and careers are unknown, Peso Pluma has often spoken about the impact that their musical tastes and upbringing had on him. 

According to the artist, he was exposed to a wide range of music at an early age, which helped shape his musical style and career.

The Importance of Early Exposure to Music

Research shows that early exposure to music can significantly impact a child’s cognitive, emotional, and social development, including language and memory skills. 

Peso Pluma’s parents’ emphasis on music helped to create an environment that nurtured their son’s artistic talents and shaped his future career path.

The Name “Peso Pluma”

Peso Pluma’s name was inspired by the featherweight boxing division. During a performance with his musicians, famed boxer Marco Antonio Barrera observed that most of them had thin skin, a trademark physical trait of boxers in the featherweight division. 

After a brief discussion, Barrera suggested the name Peso Pluma, which Laija adopted.

Collaboration with Family

While it’s unclear whether Peso Pluma has any siblings, he has collaborated musically with his cousin Roberto since his youth. 

Collaborating with family members can promote a sense of community and shared love of music, in addition to allowing for the interchange of creative ideas and influences. 

Peso Pluma’s early collaboration with his cousin may have created the foundation for his eventual success in the music business.

Peso Pluma’s Career

Pluma’s talent for music and interest in the art form started at a young age, leading him to pursue singing and songwriting. 

In 2020, his debut album, Disco en Vivo, was released by the record label El Cartel de los Nogales. He achieved international stardom in 2022 after collaborating with Luis R. Conriquez, Natanael Cano, Becky G, Nicki Nicole, and Ovy.

Peso Pluma’s first major hit came in 2022 with the release of “El Belicon,” a collaboration with singer Ral Vega. 

This song became his best-selling hit, brought him much-needed recognition, and became a staple for Latin pop music in the United States and beyond. It sold 480K copies in the United States and was certified eight times Platinum (Latin).

Since then, his success has continued, with five singles charting on the Billboard Hot 100, a career milestone for any Mexican artist. 

His song “Ella baila sola” peaked at number 17, which is the highest rank for a Mexican artist in the U.S.

Peso Pluma has also collaborated with some of the most prominent Latin urban musicians like Bad Bunny, Rafa Paboon, and Anuel AA, recording two additional albums and numerous songs.

In Conclusion

Peso Pluma’s parents continue to remain a mystery, but their influence on his musical upbringing and taste is evident. 

Exposure to music at an early age and a focus on creativity and collaboration with family members helped shape his career and led to his eventual success.

Peso Pluma’s meteoric rise in the music industry is a testament to his talent and dedication to the art form. We can only imagine that his parents played a significant role in shaping the artist he is today.
