Why Zendaya's Name Came Up In The Johnny Depp And Amber Heard Trial?

In Katherine Arnold's analysis presented to the court before testifying, the entertainment expert estimated that Johnny Depp's 2019 defamation suit that led to the trial might have cost Amber $50 million based on estimated earnings. Katherine also gave a list of comparable stars.

The people she named similar to Amber were Chris Pine, Gal Gadot, Ana de Armas, Jason Momoa, and Zendaya. Johnny's lawyer, Wayne Dennison, begged to disagree with the comparisons and argued why Zendaya could not be compared to Amber. He said, "You indicated that another person that you compared Ms. Heard with is Zendaya, a person so famous she goes by one name."

That gave fans the first reason Zendaya and Amber cannot be comparable. Not many people go by one name in Hollywood, and Zendaya is one of the few. Dennison then mentioned her accomplishments, noting, "She's been on the Disney Channel since 13 years old, she's won an Emmy, she was singing and dancing and swinging from trapezes in The Greatest Showman. She's now been in multiple Spider-Man movies." It's clear that Amber's accomplishments in the film industry are nowhere near Zendaya's achievements.

Johnny Depp's Lawyer Wayne Dennison Defended Zendaya During The Trial

Dennison went on to give another reason why Zendaya and Amber Heard should not even be compared. He said, "she's ten years younger than your clients, but this is a person you deem comparable." Hollywood's entertainment lawyer Richard Marks also took the stand and disagreed with the comparisons. He testified in court that Katherine's comments were wildly speculative.

He claimed that Amber Heard's career could not be compared to the likes of Jason Momoa and Zendaya and justified the argument by citing Momoa's role in Game of Thrones and Zendaya's strong one-name branding. When asked if Amber was comparable to the people the entertainment expert listed, Marks said, "again, they are not comparable. Jason Momoa was Aquaman, Chris Pine as Captain Kirk, Gal Gadot as Wonder Woman." Then he added, "Zendaya has been working on the Disney Channel since she was 13; she's in all the Spider-Man movies."

Richard also blasted Amber when he said she had to test or audition for the part of Mera in Aquaman, which affected her earning capacity. According to Marks, an established actor usually wouldn't test; they'd be offered the role. But Amber was in a group of actors that needed to be tested to see if the studio wanted to hire them. And then, if they hired them, they'd be locked up for potentially four movies at a very lucrative increase.
